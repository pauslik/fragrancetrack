The review and about citrus scents - I think everyone already knows how challenging they are for me.

I've had many Xerjoff samples for a long time and have occasionally tested them in small doses, but every time I have to wash them off. Now I've decided to use them and I can tell you that it has been mostly just a pain. They have scents that I like a lot, but traditional masculine citrus scents are something I don't want floating in my nose - not on my skin, nor on anyone else's. Nio was sharp citrus with the traditional combo of spices, which is exactly the worst combination for my nose. Add vetiver to that and you have a horror movie. I don't find anything attractive or pleasant about these scents at all. That's why I only wrote a short statement about it: "Sharp, bitter, boring, linear but luckily not that car freshener type and spices are moderate. Technically not so bad but still, absolutely no for me. I'm lucky that my husband doesn't like this kind of scents."

Uden is also a boring, quite ordinary fresh citrus scent, but it is saved firstly by Grapefruit, although it is not very realistic in this scent. Secondly, the scent lacks spices and vetiver. There are notes in the scent (Sandalwood and Vanilla) that soften the scent into a velvety and creamy texture, making the citrus notes more pleasant. In addition, there is a nice sweetness from rum and Vanilla, although I don't think there is any boozy accord, nor noticeable coffee aroma either. The scent is somehow fresh and yet comforting. The base is well made and the whole mix is harmonious.

Exceptionally, I only wore this scent for two full days, because despite it being a pleasant citrus scent, I don't find the scent to be my own, and so I wore it when I was at home (indoors and outdoors). The scent is suitable for both warmer and colder days. Uden is totally unisex.

I have several Xerjoff scents that are all great so, I went based on that and reviews. Honestly, this one was very disappointing mainly due to the very, very weak longevity. Especially, considering the listed concentration, price and that it's Xerjoff. I was not expecting a scent that had massive projection, scent trail, or that would fill a room like the Toni Iomi Special Monkey....but this is like a weak cologne, or lotion , barely an EDT let alone an EDP. Don't get me wrong....it smells great !....for about 1 hour. But, even in that time you have to be right up on it...very intimate space to catch a whiff. This scent transcends through all 3 layers very quickly with some of this listed notes barely discernable as they fleetingly pass by. This is a nice scent but so very subtle and fades incredibly fast. My other Xerjoff fragrances: Torino 21, Naxos and the one by Toni Iomi only require 2-3 sprays for indoors. This Uden, requires 6 sprays or more and reapplying often...and still fades out too fast on skin. I haven't tried spraying clothes yet. I can see why some reviews have resold theirs.
I did really like the included meteorite with certificate of authentication. My daughter got a kick out of that. But, it was an expensive little rock. I will not be buying Uden again.
UPDATE: the packaging, Lot and batch numbers all verified. Really doubtful this was a fake. Also compared to my other Xerjoff scents. Same, authentic everything. I contacted Xerjoff to see if I had bad Lot/ batch and to inquire if they were tracking any issues with Uden longevity issues. First question was...
" Where did you buy it from?" OK that's a valid question. But if the vendor is not on their list, or you did not buy direct...they wouldn't even help with the lot/ batch numbers verification...I did that on my own. The rep. from customer service said, "I can't help you, you must have gotten a fake". OK, if it's a fake, wouldn't you want to investigate that? It has Xerjoff all over the packaging, the cap weighs the same, labels are all good, even has the meteorite included. Nope.
OK, let's say it's not a fake. The longevity is 1 hour tops. So, is this a bad batch lot, bottle? She wouldn't budge. I offered to send pictures, and a decant a sample. Nope. So, if it's counterfeit, Xerjoff doesn't care? If it's a bad bottle, batch, lot...Xerjoff doesn't care either? Nope, She said someone could gave replaced the contents of the bottle.
I'm done with Xerjoff. The fancy presentation, the high prices, and they don't care if you didn't buy it where they want you to at top dollar, THE END
